The Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) Cycle

Implementing an Information Security Management System (ISMS) is an iterative process. The PDCA cycle helps you continuously improve your security posture.

1. Plan

Establish the ISMS by defining the scope, policies, objectives, risk assessment process, and a Statement of Applicability. This sets the foundation for your security program.

2. Do

Implement the plan. This involves applying the selected security controls, conducting security awareness training, and managing the day-to-day operations of the ISMS.

3. Check

Monitor and review the ISMS. Conduct internal audits, measure the effectiveness of controls, and regularly review risk assessments to ensure the system is working as intended.

4. Act

Continuously improve the ISMS. Take corrective and preventive actions based on the results of your monitoring and reviews. Update policies and controls to adapt to new threats.
